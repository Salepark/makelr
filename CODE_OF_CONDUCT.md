# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, as contributors and maintainers of Makelr, pledge to make participation in our community a respectful, safe, and constructive experience for everyone.

We are committed to fostering an open, welcoming, and harassment-free environment, regardless of:

- Experience level
- Gender identity and expression
- Sexual orientation
- Disability
- Personal appearance
- Body size
- Race, ethnicity, or religion
- Nationality
- Technical opinions or product philosophy

This project exists to help people build and own their own automation workflows.  
We expect discussions and collaborations to reflect that spirit: practical, respectful, and focused on building.

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Showing empathy towards other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, insults, or personal attacks
- Trolling, deliberate provocation, or disruptive behavior
- Publishing othersâ€™ private information without permission
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and discussions
- Code reviews and comments
- Project-related chats or forums
- Any other place where the Makelr community is represented

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

They may take appropriate action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, including:

- Warning the contributor
- Temporarily or permanently banning a contributor
- Closing or rejecting issues or pull requests
- Removing content that violates this Code of Conduct

Decisions by maintainers are made to protect the community and the project.

---

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by contacting the project maintainers.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.  
We will respect confidentiality and prioritize the safety of the community.
